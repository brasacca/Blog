Entry 2: Importance of Accessability in Web Development (9/9/2021)

Technology is meant to make people's lives easier, and in order to maintain that precedent then those working in web development need to make sure that web sites are made accessible to everyone, regardless of physical or cognitive abilities. Examples of this include screen readers for those who are visually impaired, captions on videos for those who are have hearing impairments. Additionally, making your website accessable will also benefit others, including those who use mobile devices or who have slow internet connection.

There are many practices in HTMl, CSS and JavaScript that can provide accessability to a web site. Here are a few examples:

The <abbr> element that allows an abbreviation, acronym, or initialization to be associated with its expansion

<p>Web content is marked up using <abbr title="Hypertext Markup Language">HTML</abbr>.</p>

How you display links on a web site is also an opportunity to boost your site's accessability. There should be color variation between the standard link color, when the link has been visited, and when the link is activated. Also, keyboard accessability is important for those who have physical disabilities, so when navigating via tabbing, the link should have a border around it to indicate it is being focused on.
